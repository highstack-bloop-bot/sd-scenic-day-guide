The fun one — climb a log-and-rope ladder, follow a canyon ledge to "the Notch" for a window view over the White River valley. Skip in wet/slick conditions.

9:45 PM

Devils Tower

America's first national monument — an 867-ft volcanic monolith. Covered by your $80 pass. You'll arrive under stars, not sunset (see below).

⚠ Heads-up: you won't catch Devils Tower by sunset With Rushmore in the plan you reach Devils Tower ~9:45 PM — roughly an hour past a summer sunset (~8:50 PM). The Rushmore detour south, then back northwest into Wyoming, is what eats the light — not Wall Drug.

To catch the sunset instead: drop Mount Rushmore and drive Badlands → Devils Tower directly (~2.5 hr from the Pinnacles gate). Leaving Badlands by ~6:00 PM puts you at the Tower ~8:30 PM, just in time. You can't do both Rushmore and a Devils Tower sunset in one afternoon.

Two Ways To Run The Day

pick based on how early you'll start
Recommended

A · Badlands sunrise, then loop the Hills

  1. Pre-dawn drive to Badlands (~1 hr from Rapid City)
  2. Badlands sunrise: Big Badlands → Notch → Panorama → Yellow Mounds → Pinnacles
  3. Drive west ~1.5 hr toward Keystone
  4. Mount Rushmore (late morning — faces well lit)
  5. Iron Mountain Rd southbound (tunnels frame Rushmore)
  6. Custer: Wildlife Loop bison → Sylvan Lake → Needles Hwy
  7. Wind Cave prairie & Rankin Ridge (late afternoon)
≈ 5.5–6.5 hrs driving + stops = a full 12–14 hr day. Low light makes Badlands most dramatic and beats the heat.
Tighter

B · Skip Badlands, savour the Black Hills

  1. Mount Rushmore
  2. Iron Mountain Road
  3. Wildlife Loop (bison)
  4. Sylvan Lake
  5. Needles Highway (Needles Eye, Cathedral Spires)
  6. Wind Cave NP
A relaxed ~3.5–4 hr driving loop. Badlands becomes its own half-day trip another time — no rushing.

The Two Byways To Prioritise

the scenic heart of the day

Needles Highway · SD-87

14 miles · no large RVs · slow-going

Threads through towering granite "needle" spires with hairpin turns and the tight Needles Eye Tunnel (~8'9" wide), ending at postcard Sylvan Lake. The single most spectacular short drive in the region.

Iron Mountain Road · US-16A

~17 miles · drive north→south

Tunnels deliberately cut to frame Mount Rushmore as you emerge, plus corkscrew "pigtail" bridges and 314° switchbacks. North-to-south gives the best Rushmore reveals.
